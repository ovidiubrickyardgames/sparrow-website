# Copywriting Frameworks

Use these as skeletons, not scripts — fill them with specifics from the actual brief, never generic placeholders.

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
Best for: ads, sales pages, single-CTA pages.
- **Attention**: A hook that stops the scroll — a surprising fact, a sharp question, a bold claim you can back up.
- **Interest**: Make it about the reader's specific situation. Why should *this* person keep reading?
- **Desire**: Paint the outcome. Make the benefit feel real and close, not abstract.
- **Action**: One clear next step, stated plainly.

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution
Best for: cold emails, problem-aware audiences, short-form ads.
- **Problem**: Name the reader's problem in their own language.
- **Agitate**: Show the cost of not solving it — time, money, embarrassment, risk. Don't manufacture fear that isn't real; surface a true cost.
- **Solution**: Introduce the fix as the natural resolution, not a hard pivot.

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge
Best for: transformation-driven products (courses, coaching, fitness, software that changes a workflow).
- **Before**: The reader's current, frustrating state.
- **After**: The specific, vivid state after using the product.
- **Bridge**: The product is what gets them from Before to After — name the mechanism.

## 4 Ps — Picture, Promise, Prove, Push
Best for: sales pages needing more proof-heavy persuasion.
- **Picture**: Paint the desired end state.
- **Promise**: State exactly what the product delivers and by when.
- **Prove**: Testimonials, data, guarantees, credentials.
- **Push**: Urgency/CTA — deadline, scarcity, or simply a clear next step.

## FAB — Features, Advantages, Benefits
Best for: product descriptions, spec-heavy products, B2B.
- **Feature**: What it is/has.
- **Advantage**: What that feature does mechanically.
- **Benefit**: What that means for the reader's life/work — always end here, this is what actually persuades.
Example: "12-hour battery (feature) → runs a full workday without a charger (advantage) → one less cable in your bag, one less thing to remember (benefit)."

## Choosing a framework
- Cold outreach, skeptical/unaware reader → PAS
- Reader already wants a transformation, just needs to believe it's possible → BAB
- High-consideration purchase needing trust-building → 4 Ps
- Feature-dense product (software, hardware, B2B tools) → FAB, then wrap in AIDA for the overall page
- When in doubt or for short-form (ads, single emails) → AIDA
